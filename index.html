Resumé

Why Art Therapy?

Art therapy focuses on inner experiences that emerge through the artmaking process, and elicits an individual's inherent capacity for creation. Using art media, individuals can explore emotional conflicts, manage behavior and addictions, develop social skills, improve reality orientation, reduce anxiety, and increase self-esteem. The goal in all art therapy practice is to improve or restore a client’s functioning and his or her sense of personal well-being.


The American Art Therapy Association (AATA) is the national organization of art therapists since 1969, and requires that all art therapists receive a Master’s Degree with required classes in psychology and studio art. In New York state, art therapists must be licensed as Creative Art Therapists (LCAT) to practice in full. Using a variety of clinical and creative techniques, art therapists develop relationships with clients utilizing art material as a vessel and catalyst for self-expression (verbal or otherwise).

My Approach

I have 5 years experience in the field of behavioral health practicing psychotherapy with adult and young adult populations. I take a multimodal, person-centered, trauma informed approach to mental health treatment for individuals with serious mental illness and substance abuse history.

The individualized treatment plans I create for my clients helps them to assess and meet their goals concerning medication management, daily living skills, symptom management, and progress documentation.

Group therapy environments can help clients to gain crucial social support during the therapeutic process. I run multiple groups designed especially for distinct circumstances, including substance abuse recovery.
